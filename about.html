Professional Summary

Over the past 15+ years, my journey in information security has been shaped by one consistent goal: building environments that are secure, resilient, and capable of supporting real business growth.

I started my career deep in the networking layer — routing, switching, and infrastructure troubleshooting. Over time, that foundation expanded into the broader security ecosystem: firewalls, VPNs, proxies, CASB, SASE, segmentation, load balancing, and Zero Trust architectures. Each layer added a new perspective on how modern enterprises protect their systems while maintaining performance and scalability.

Working on the Enterprise side, i translated business and regulatory requirements into practical, risk-aligned security solutions. Security is rarely just a technical decision; it sits at the intersection of risk management, compliance, operations, and business priorities. A large part of my work has involved bridging these worlds — turning governance and risk expectations into architectures that engineers can implement and businesses can rely on.

Along the way, I have had the opportunity to work with cross-functional teams, guide technical implementations, and strengthen security posture across complex infrastructures. These experiences reinforced an important lesson: security is most effective when it is integrated into the system design rather than added as an afterthought.

Today, my focus remains on designing secure, scalable architectures that support operational continuity while enabling organizations to move forward with confidence.
